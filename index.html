MY RÉSUMÉ

Why the interest in Biology/Biomechanics?

Family

As a child of two doctors and a sibling to two U of T Life Science graduates, I have experienced a very healthcare-oriented upbringing. Accompanied with excellent marks in the maths and sciences throughout secondary and post-secondary schooling, I've found a passion in the development and design of technology in the medical field.

Social Outreach

Having experienced working with not-for-profit organizations (such as FreetheChildren, the Canadian Cancer Society, and LiveDifferent) as well as running/attending social-justice clubs (MINGA, Engineers Without Borders) and conferences, and going on mission trips, I have been touched by the people who may not be as fortunate as we are here in Canada. These experiences fuel my passion and desire to design and build solutions that make life better.

How do I plan to reach my goals?

iGEM | International Genetically Engineered Machine Competition

iGEM is a competion focusing on innovations in synthetic biology. Waterloo's chapter has been consistently performing well and I hope to join this student design team in the near future as a member of either the Lab and Design or Mathematical Modelling team.

Undergraduate Research Assistant

Having qualified for a research assistantship, and having a keen interest in research and general learning, I have made arrangements for the upcoming term to work with a professor and her work with the University of Waterloo’s school of Optometry and Vision Science.

Global Engineering Solution

In the fall of 2015, I worked with a team to design a product meant to sanitize drinking water in Arroyo Seco, Dominican Republic. Adopting the iterative and user-centered design processes, my team and I designed and prototyped a modular filtration/storage unit that met enginnering specifications and user requirements.
